The Ultimate Guide to Trekking the Annapurna Circuit

Prakash Gurung

Senior Trekking Expert • Updated Oct 15, 2024

Share

Considered one of the greatest treks in the world, the Annapurna Circuit offers an unparalleled journey through diverse landscapes—from subtropical forests to high-altitude deserts. This guide covers everything you need to know before lacing up your boots.

1. Understanding the Route

The classic circuit spans roughly 160 to 230 kilometers (100-145 miles) depending on where motor roads are used and where the trek is started and ended. The trail crosses two different river valleys and encircles the Annapurna Massif. The highest point is Thorong La Pass at an elevation of 5,416m (17,769 ft).

The classic counter-clockwise route helps significantly with acclimatization.

2. When to Go

The best times to trek are during the pre-monsoon (spring) and post-monsoon (autumn) seasons.

  • Autumn (Oct-Nov): Clear skies, excellent visibility, and stable weather. This is peak season, so expect crowded trails.
  • Spring (Mar-May): Blooming rhododendrons and warmer days. Occasional haze can obscure lower views, but high-altitude vistas remain spectacular.

3. Essential Gear and Preparation

Packing smart is crucial. While teahouses provide blankets, a good sleeping bag (rated to -10°C) is essential for higher elevations. Layering is key, as temperatures fluctuate wildly between the humid lowlands and the freezing pass. Don't forget sturdy, broken-in trekking boots and reliable water purification methods.

"The challenge of Thorong La is mental as much as physical. Take your time, hydrate, and listen to your body." — Prakash Gurung
